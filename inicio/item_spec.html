• item_action - This is for an action that is performed on an expense or income, or something that the expense or income itself does to an agent.

I pay 500 in rent
rent costs me 500
I earn 300 a week

  • item_activity - This is for something the user, or someone else, does to generate income or expenditure.

I've been renting this flat for 500 a month

  • item_actual - This is the basic tag for the thing the user actually says they spend their money on or earn their money from:

rent is 500

  • item_agent - This is the person (almost certainly a person) who is responsible for some income or expenditure activity.

my wife earns 1000 a month
my husband spends 50 a week at the pub

  • item_beneficiary - This is for a person (usually not a thing) that is the target of income or expenditure.

I spend 50 a month on food for my kids
I pay 200 towards my mum's care home bill

  • item_container - This is for super-items that are expressed as having key item values as components.

I spend 50 on gas, as part of my utilities

  • item_excluded - This is for something that is explicitly mentioned as not part of an expenditure or income being expressed.

I spend 600 a month on rent, but that doesn't include utilities

  • item_from - This is for an entity from which money is received.

my sister chips in Â£50 a month for the utilities

  • item_included - This is for the text that describes things that are part of an item that is the basis for income or expenditure:

my rent is 500 and that includes internet

  • item_instrument - This is for the things which facilitate making payments of getting paid.

I put 10 a week on the metre for electricity
I've got a direct debit of 200 a month set up for utilities

  • item_null - This is for when a user is saying they don't spend anything on an item.

I don't pay rent

  • item_object - This is a thing (not, generally, a person) that is in some sense the target of or basis for income or expenditure.

I've been renting this flat for 500 a month
food for my dog costs 20 a week

  • item_reason - This is for something that is either the cause or the goal of an instance of income or expenditure:

I don't have a wage because I'm retired
I have diabetes so I buy blood sugar monitors

  • item_rebate - This is for a mechanism that in some sense or another scales or increments income or expenditure.

I make 20000 before taxes
my rent is 500 a month, but the council covers part of that

  • item_recipient - This is a person or entity to which money is paid.

I give my parents 100 a month for room and board

  • item_specification - This is for things that are (possibly collectively) the entirety of the values for an item that are being expressed in an input.

I pay 100 for utilities, but that only covers gas and electricity

  • item_unsure - This is for when a user is saying they don't know what (or whether) they spend anything on a particular item.

I don't know what I pay on rent

  • item_venue - This is for the place where some income or expenditure activity happens.

my weekly shop at Tesco comes to 50
my meals at work cost 30 a week

  • item_wrong - This is for when a user is saying that an established value for a particular item is incorrect.

what you have for rent is wrong
